Project Description

Inspiration Behind Our Project

Knowing that heart disease has a large impact on such a big percentage of the human population, as well as witnessing the recent discoveries of biomarkers and the molecular components of plaque buildup, we wanted to take the initiative towards lowering the CVD rate of mortality. The first step in doing this is earlier detection.

Abstract

Atherosclerosis, an inflammatory disease characterized by plaque buildup in the inner arterial walls, is a common cardiovascular disease affecting millions in America and worldwide. However current detection methods are costly, time-consuming, and often not used during medical visits unless individuals show signs of further cardiovascular complications. Here, we describe a method of early atherosclerosis detection centered around the detection of oxidized low-density lipoprotein (oxLDL), a biomarker in the bloodstream whose presence is associated with early to middle stages of atherosclerosis development. This synthetic biology approach provides individuals with a quick, accessible, and proactive approach to testing their likelihood of having atherosclerosis by using SHuffle E.coli to produce oxLDL antibodies and allowing the detection of oxLDL levels to be read from an easy-to-read test strip. This method is projected to significantly reduce the cost of current detection methods and add a dimension to preexisting biomarker detection diagnostics.
