Types of Cardiovascular Diseases

Arrhythmia is a condition where the heart beats at an irregular pace. It affects about 5% of the American population. The causes of this disease vary, but typically include heavy smoking or alcohol use, an excess intake of caffeine or other stimulants, stress, thyroid disease, fever, and hereditary patterns. The severity of arrhythmia varies as well, with cases falling on a spectrum between completely harmless to life threatening. Risk factors for arrhythmia include coronary artery disease, and it is highly recommended that people reduce their risk of developing arrhythmia by engaging in heart-healthy activities. (LINK TO CITE: https://www.scripps.org/sparkle-assets/documents/heart_rhythm_facts.pdf)

Congenital heart diseases (CHDs) result from abnormalities in the development of the heart that arise before birth. Nearly 1% of babies born in the US are diagnosed with a CHD annually. Heart defects can range from underdeveloped structures of the heart such as valves and chambers, to the switched position of major arteries. The cause of congenital heart disease is unknown, and corrective surgery is often needed for children born with a defect to live healthily into adulthood. Research suggests that children born with a CHD could be at a higher risk of developing coronary artery diseases including atherosclerosis later in life. Individuals with CHD should monitor their heart health and take effort to live a healthy lifestyle. Examples of this are to eat a balanced diet, stay physically active unless advised otherwise by a doctor, avoid smoking and street drugs, and maintain a reasonable weight. (LINK TO CITE: https://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/heartdefects/data.html https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/joim.13080 https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/congenital-heart-defects/care-and-treatment-for-congenital-heart-defects/heart-health-recommendations-for-those-with-chd)

Inspiration Behind Our Project

Knowing that heart disease has a large impact on such a big percentage of the human population, as well as witnessing the recent discoveries of biomarkers and the molecular components of plaque buildup, we wanted to take the initiative towards lowering the CVD rate of mortality. The first step in doing this is earlier detection.
