Overview of Cardiovascular Disease

Cardiovascular disease is a broad category of conditions that affect the structure and function of the heart and its surrounding vasculature. Common cardiovascular diseases include heart disease, heart attack, stroke, heart failure, arrhythmia, and heart valve problems. [#] According to the Center for Disease Control and Prevention, heart disease is the leading cause of death in men, women, and people of the majority of racial and ethnic groups in the US. To put this into numbers, one in every five deaths in 2020, a year notoriously known for the COVID pandemic, was attributed to cardiovascular disease. [#]

Behavioral Risk Factors of Cardiovascular Disease (CITE World Health Organization 2021)

  • Lack of physical activity
  • Tobacco use
  • Harmful use of alcohol

How do these factors show up in an individual?

  • Obesity/Overweight
  • Increased blood pressure
  • Diabetes
  • High cholesterol

Types of Cardiovascular Diseases

There are three main types of CVDs, coronary heart disease, arrhythmia, and congenital heart disease.

A ha health-topics

Arrhythmia is a condition where the heart beats at an irregular pace. It affects about 5% of the American population. [###] The causes of this disease vary, but typically include heavy smoking or alcohol use, an excess intake of caffeine or other stimulants, stress, thyroid disease, fever, and hereditary patterns. The severity of arrhythmia varies as well, with cases falling on a spectrum between completely harmless to life threatening. Risk factors for arrhythmia include coronary artery disease, and it is highly recommended that people reduce their risk of developing arrhythmia by engaging in heart-healthy activities.

Congenital heart diseases (CHDs) result from abnormalities in the development of the heart that arise before birth. Nearly 1% of babies born in the US are diagnosed with a CHD annually. Heart defects can range from underdeveloped structures of the heart such as valves and chambers, to the switched position of major arteries. [# CITE THIS] The cause of congenital heart disease is unknown, and corrective surgery is often needed for children born with a defect to live healthily into adulthood. Research suggests that children born with a CHD could be at a higher risk of developing coronary artery diseases including atherosclerosis later in life. [# CITE THIS] Individuals with CHD should monitor their heart health and take effort to live a healthy lifestyle. Examples of this are to eat a balanced diet, stay physically active unless advised otherwise by a doctor, avoid smoking and street drugs, and maintain a reasonable weight. [# CITE THIS]

Atherosclerosis

Atherosclerosis is a chronic inflammatory disease of the large arteries. It is the root cause of many CVDs such as myocardial infarction or stroke.

Pathogenesis

Atherosclerotic lesions are caused by endothelial cell damage, which causes low density lipoproteins (LDL) to enter the intima [3]. LDL is very prone to oxidation from molecules released by damaged cells. OxLDL in high quantities is toxic and causes more inflammation. Inflammation attracts monocytes to the lesion and the monocytes migrate into the intima under the influence of chemokines [6]. As monocytes enter the intima, they differentiate into macrophages and engulf oxLDL, becoming foam cells. The foam cells undergo apoptosis and release lipid content, creating lipid necrotic cores [7]. A fibrous cap of collagen and elastin, produced by smooth muscle cells (SMCs), forms over the necrotic core, attempting to heal the lesion [8]. Lesions with a thin cap are more likely to rupture and thus less stable, leading to blockage of blood vessels and CVD [9]. After atherosclerosis lesions rupture, thrombus forms and chemokines play an important role in platelets activation and aggregation [10].

The first step in doing this is earlier detection.
