Attributions

Debt of Gratitude

AtheroSHuffle is not the work of one person, but a whole team endeavor including our team members, our instructor, and our sponsors. Our team members each filled their own roles and contributed to an incredible team dynamic which allowed us to persevere in the toughest of times. Our instructor Dr. Kozminski taught us what we needed, guided us through the process of iGEM, and helped to secure the funds needed to make our project possible. Finally, our sponsors are the source of funds integral to the success of our project, and very little would have been possible without them. We would like to thank each and every one of the names listed below for making the 2022 Virginia iGEM AtheroSHuffle project possible.

Team Attributions

Dr. Keith Kozminski

Dr. Kozminski is the Virginia iGEM 2022 faculty mentor. He taught us experimental protocols, gave us lab training, guided us through technical difficulties, and inspired us persevere during the trying times we faced throughout our project.

Yilun Zhou

Yilun is the iGEM Virginia 2022 Team Lead. She was involved in developing the original project design and was a lead on the Wet Lab committee. She researched, documented, and taught the team experimental protocols. She also had the important role of uniting and organizing the team. Her leadership and efforts were invaluable to our project, and we would not be as strong as we are as a team without her.

Miranda Khoury

Miranda is the Wet Lab committee lead. She oversaw and greatly informed the workflow of the lab, troubleshooting, and experiment design. Miranda also was the primary member involved in engineering our antibody sequences. She coordinated frequently with Modeling to improve the productivity of Wet Lab. She also started journal clubs where the whole team would convene to discuss scientific literature to improve general knowledge related to our project. Her drive, initiative, and consistency are inspiring.

Ivory Tang

Ivory is the Modeling Lead for our team as well as a Wet Lab secondary. Ivory organized and led the two major projects for our modeling team this summer, those being protein modeling and developing an advection-dispersion-reaction equation to quantify our LFA design. She is highly motivated and taught herself skills such as using the supercomputer to develop protein models so she could teach the rest of the team and further the development of our project.

Isha Patel

Isha is the Human Practices and Outreach committee lead. She searched for and contacted experts, helped get sponsors, planned our project implementation, and managed our social media presence to facilitate public education. She also assisted with running wet lab experiments. One of her biggest highlights is kickstarting a podcast to interview health professionals and creating heart-healthy recipe videos on social media to spread awareness about cardiovascular disease. Her creativity and interpersonal skills are precious.

Peneeta Wojcik

Peneeta is the Wiki lead for our team and was largely involved in the wet lab and modeling. Peneeta spearheaded the foundation of our wiki from early on to prevent a last-minute wiki rush during the fall semester. She coded and designed the website by teaching herself web development skills and the use of libraries such as Bootstrap and WebGL. She is the core of our wiki development. She also edited the project promotional video.

Jayati Maram

Jayati supports the Modeling, Wet Lab, Integrated Human Practices, Finance, and Wiki committees. She conducted interviews with professionals for the IHP podcast, helped write grant applications, created our survey, created the app demo, and helped conduct wet lab experiments. Her many talents have been invaluable to our team and to the development of our project. She is a dedicated individual who is always available to help the team wherever she can.

Dr. Keith Kozminski

We owe an enormous debt of gratitude to our instructor Dr. Keith Kozminski for both getting us involved in the iGEM competition and guiding us along the way. Dr. Kosminski taught the Synthetic Biology class we were required to take in order to participate in iGEM; there we learned what synthetic biology is, how the iGEM competitions work, and drafted project proposals. He constantly helped us troubleshoot our problems and provided us with necessary reagents. He also secured the funds and grants necessary for us to enter the iGEM competition and travel to Paris, France for the Grand Jamboree.
