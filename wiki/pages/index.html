AtheroSHuffle

A lateral flow test strip for proactive atherosclerosis detection.

A Worldwide Health Crisis

Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the United States and around the world.
Each year, one in four Americans die of various cardiovascular complications. [1] Atherosclerosis is a form of heart disease characterized by plaque buildup in the inner arterial wall. It progresses through multiple stages and is often caught too late. Add more health facts here.

Pathogenesis

Oxidized low density lipoprotein (OxLDL) concentration increases in the blood, contributing to plaque formation. As plaque buildup grows, blood flow through the affected arteries is constricted. [3] Blood vessels begin to narrow and blood flow decreases throughout the body. This can be fatal, leading to further cardiovascular complications including blood clots, vessel rupture, osteoarthritis, aneurysms, stroke, and heart attack. [4] Research supports that atherosclerosis begins developing when people are in their 20s, but treatment does not begin until ages 50-75. [6]

Problems with Current Treatment Methods

Currently, physicians use electrocardiogram (EKG) data analysis, CT scans, and angiograms to detect plaque, which can range between $270 to $4700. These methods are expensive and require multiple visits to health professionals to interpret test results. Diagnostic tests for cardiovascular disease are not included in routine checkups and are not recommended until an individual shows signs of further cardiovascular complications.

Our Solution

To address the need for proactive atherosclerosis testing, we created AtheroSHuffle, a a test strip that uses antibodies synthesized by the E. coli SHuffle strain to detect OxLDL in blood samples. Our test strip consists of a labelled antibody, an scFv fragment, and a nanobody. The labelled antibody binds to OxLDL in a blood sample and forms a sandwich complex with the scFv antibody. The nanobody binds to the labelled antibody as a control line.

The Synthetic Biology Puzzle Piece

To synthesize our antibodies, we chose to use the engineered E. coli strand SHuffle. This strand is unique because it has the ability to form disulfide bonds during protein synthesis, which are needed to stabilize antibody structure. [CITATION] Typically, these antibodies would be produced by inoculating animals and pulling blood or spleen samples, which is harmful and invasive to the animals. By introducing our antibodies in E. coli SHuffle, they can be produced in a safe and humane manner.
