A Worldwide Health Crisis

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) is the leading cause of death worldwide, making up 32% of total deaths. This umbrella term refers to several diseases including stroke, coronary heart disease, and other conditions affecting blood vessels.

Economic Burden

In addition to its health impacts, CVDs are extremely costly. Current diagnostics for cardiovascular disease are not cheap, and when bundled with treatment, the direct cost of care increases by another 85 percent. National expenditures were at $126 billion in 2015 and are expected to increase to $309 billion in 2035.

How We're Making a Difference

Our device targets Atherosclerosis, an inflammatory disease characterized by plaque build-up in the inner arterial walls. It is a common cardiovascular disease affecting millions in America and worldwide. Current detection methods are costly, time-consuming, and not recommended during medical visits unless individuals show signs of further cardiovascular complications.
Our focus is developing a method atherosclerosis detection centered around the detection of oxidized low-density lipoprotein (oxLDL), a biomarker in the bloodstream whose presence is associated with early to middle stages of atherosclerosis development. This synthetic biology approach provides individuals with a quick, accessible, and proactive method to testing their likelihood of having atherosclerosis. By engineering the SHuffle E. coli strand to produce oxLDL-specific antibodies, oxLDL levels can be determined from an easy-to-read test strip containing these antibodies. This method is projected to significantly reduce the cost of current detection methods and add a dimension to preexisting biomarker detection diagnostics.
