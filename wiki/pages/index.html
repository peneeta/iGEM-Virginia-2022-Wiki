AtheroSHuffle

A Novel Point-of-Care Detection for Early Atherosclerosis

Our Mission

“AtheroSHuffle represents a collaboration between science and society to proactively combat the complications of cardiovascular health.”

A Worldwide Health Crisis

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) is the leading cause of death worldwide, making up 32% of total deaths. This umbrella term refers to several diseases including stroke, coronary heart disease, and other conditions affecting blood vessels.

Economic Burden

In addition to its health impacts, CVDs are extremely costly. In a technical report prepared for the American Heart Association, combined costs of CVD detection tests and hospital visits are expected to reach 750 billion dollars by 2035.

Projected costs of CVD from 2015 to 2035. Data obtained from Research Triangle Institute (RTI) technical report. #

Despite the rising diagnoses and death tolls, an estimated 80% of CVDs can be prevented if they are detected earlier in life #.

How We're Making a Difference

There is no method on the market for early detection, and late detection is extremely costly and invasive. To combat these problems, we developed AtheroSHuffle, test strip that detects early signs of heart disease. Our device targets atherosclerosis, which is characterized by plaque build-up in the inner arterial walls.

The strip detects oxidized LDL (OxLDL), a biomarker associated with early to middle stages of atherosclerosis development. We used synthetic biology to engineer a stain of E. coli called SHuffle to produce OxLDL-specific antibodies. These antibodies, when adhered to the strip, allow OxLDL levels to be determined by the naked eye.

This method is projected to significantly reduce the cost of current detection methods and add a dimension to preexisting biomarker detection diagnostics.
