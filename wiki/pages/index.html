AtheroSHuffle

A lateral flow test strip for proactive atherosclerosis detection.

A Worldwide Health Crisis

Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the United States and around the world. Each year, one in four Americans die of various cardiovascular complications. [1]

Atherosclerosis is a form of heart disease characterized by plaque-buildup in the inner arterial wall. As buildups grow, blood flow through affected arteries is constricted. [3] The narrowing of blood vessels and resulting reduction of blood flow throughout the body can be fatal, leading to further cardiovascular complications including blood clots, osteoarthritis, aneurysms, stroke, and heart attack. [4]

This disease progresses through multiple stages. Research suggests that the disease begins developing when people are in their 20s, but treatment does not begin until ages 50-75. [6]

Current State-of-the-Art

Though atherosclerosis can be treated through a healthy diet and medication, people do not realize its development in their body until years after the buildup has begun. This makes reversal of the condition extremely difficult, if not impossible.

Current detection methods include electrocardiogram (EKG) data analysis, CT scans, and angiograms which can range between $270 to $4700. These methods are expensive and require a medical visit and health professionals to interpret test results.

Diagnostic methods are not included in routine checkups and are not recommended until an individual shows signs of further cardiovascular complications.

Our Solution

To address the need for proactive atherosclerosis diagnostic testing, we designed a cost-efficient and easily-accessible lateral flow test strip that tests oxLDL levels using a genetically engineered E. coli strain, SHuffle to synthesize antibodies that bind to atherosclerosis biomarkers.

The E. coli SHuffle strain is unique in that it can form disulfide bonds needed for antibody synthesis.
