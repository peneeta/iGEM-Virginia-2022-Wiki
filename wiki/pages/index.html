Abstract

Atherosclerosis, an inflammatory disease characterized by plaque build-up in the inner arterial walls, is a common cardiovascular disease affecting millions in America and worldwide. However, current detection methods are costly, time-consuming, and not recommended during medical visits unless individuals show signs of further cardiovascular complications. Here, we describe a method of early atherosclerosis detection centered around the detection of oxidized low-density lipoprotein (oxLDL), a biomarker in the bloodstream whose presence is associated with early to middle stages of atherosclerosis development. This synthetic biology approach provides individuals with a quick, accessible, and proactive method to testing their likelihood of having atherosclerosis. By engineering the SHuffle E. coli strand to produce oxLDL-specific antibodies, oxLDL levels can be determined from an easy-to-read test strip containing these antibodies. This method is projected to significantly reduce the cost of current detection methods and add a dimension to preexisting biomarker detection diagnostics.
